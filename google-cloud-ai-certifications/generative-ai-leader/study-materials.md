# Study Materials – Generative AI Leader

_Last Updated: November 30, 2025_

**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Suggested timeline:** 2–4 weeks (5–8 hours per week)

This document outlines the core resources and topics you need to master for the GenAI Leader exam, focused on decision-making and business outcomes rather than implementation details.

## Core Learning Resources

### Google Cloud Skills Boost Premium (Essential)

- [ ] "Generative AI Learning Path" – foundational GenAI concepts, terminology, and Google Cloud offerings
- [ ] "Generative AI for Leaders" or similar executive-focused course
- [ ] Vertex AI Studio demos and Model Garden overview
- [ ] Case study modules on GenAI adoption in enterprises

### Official Google Cloud Documentation

- [ ] Vertex AI overview and GenAI capabilities
- [ ] Model Garden and available foundation models (Gemini, PaLM, etc.)
- [ ] Responsible AI principles and governance documentation
- [ ] AI Principles and ethical considerations for GenAI deployments

### Practical Exploration

- [ ] Spend time in **Vertex AI Studio**: experiment with prompts, model selection, and response generation
- [ ] Review **Model Garden** for available models and their use cases
- [ ] Read case studies from industries you work in (healthcare, finance, consulting)

## Topics to Master

### GenAI Foundations (Week 1)

- [ ] Foundation models and large language models (LLMs)
- [ ] Tokens, context windows, and sequence length limitations
- [ ] Pre-training, fine-tuning, and in-context learning
- [ ] Embeddings and vector search basics
- [ ] Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) at a high level

### Google Cloud GenAI Offerings (Week 1–2)

- [ ] Vertex AI platform: Workbench, Training, Endpoints, Pipelines, Feature Store
- [ ] **Gemini** family of models and their capabilities
- [ ] Model Garden: curated, ready-to-use models for various tasks
- [ ] Vertex AI Search and Conversation: RAG-style experiences
- [ ] Duet AI integration in Google Cloud console

### Business and ROI (Week 2)

- [ ] Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for GenAI projects
- [ ] Time-to-value and quick wins vs long-term strategic bets
- [ ] Risk and compliance considerations
- [ ] Competitive advantages and market positioning

### Responsible AI & Governance (Week 2–3)

- [ ] Google Cloud AI Principles (accountability, transparency, fairness, etc.)
- [ ] Bias detection and mitigation strategies
- [ ] Data privacy and security in GenAI
- [ ] Model explainability and interpretability
- [ ] Regulatory compliance (GDPR, healthcare regulations, etc.)

### Practical Topics (Week 3–4)

- [ ] Prompt engineering: system prompts, few-shot learning, chain-of-thought
- [ ] Model selection: larger models vs smaller models, fine-tuning vs RAG
- [ ] Safety filters, guardrails, and content filtering
- [ ] Performance vs cost trade-offs
- [ ] Change management and adoption strategies

## How to Use This Material

1. **Week 1:** Complete foundational Skills Boost courses and read `genai-fundamentals.md`.
2. **Week 2:** Study `vertex-ai-overview.md` and `business-use-cases.md` in parallel with Vertex AI Studio exploration.
3. **Week 3:** Deep dive into `responsible-ai.md` and map concepts to your healthcare/consultancy scenarios.
4. **Week 4:** Review `practice-questions.md` and refine your understanding of business implications.

**Cross-references:** Complement this material with `../shared-resources/gcp-fundamentals.md` for GCP context.
